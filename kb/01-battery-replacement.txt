SALTO Electronic Lock - Battery Replacement Guide
==================================================

WHEN TO REPLACE BATTERIES
- The lock flashes red or amber repeatedly when a card is presented.
- Guests report the lock responding slowly or not responding at all.
- ProAccess SPACE shows a low-battery warning for the door.
- The lock makes a weak or unusual clicking sound instead of unlocking.

WHAT YOU NEED
- 4x AA alkaline batteries (do NOT use rechargeable or lithium batteries).
- A Phillips-head screwdriver (some models use a Torx T-20 screw).

STEP-BY-STEP REPLACEMENT

1. Open the lock's interior cover plate. On most SALTO models, this is on
   the inside of the door. Remove the screw(s) holding the cover in place.

2. Carefully pull the cover away from the door to expose the battery
   compartment.

3. Remove the old batteries. Note the direction of the positive (+) and
   negative (-) ends before removing them.

4. Insert 4 new AA alkaline batteries. Match the direction markings inside
   the compartment. All four batteries must be the same brand and type.

5. Replace the cover plate and tighten the screw(s). Do not overtighten.

6. Wait about 5 seconds for the lock to initialize.

TESTING AFTER REPLACEMENT

7. Present a known working keycard to the lock. The lock should flash green
   and unlock smoothly.

8. Lock the door again and test a second time to confirm.

9. If the lock still does not respond, remove the batteries, wait 10
   seconds, and reinsert them.

10. Check ProAccess SPACE to confirm the low-battery warning has cleared.

IMPORTANT NOTES
- Always replace all 4 batteries at the same time. Never mix old and new.
- Dispose of old batteries properly according to your property's policy.
- If the lock still does not work after fresh batteries, escalate to Tier 2
  support.
