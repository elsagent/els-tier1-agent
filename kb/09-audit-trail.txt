SALTO ProAccess SPACE - Viewing the Audit Trail
=================================================

The audit trail is a log of every event at a door: who opened it, when,
and whether access was granted or denied. This is useful for security
investigations, guest complaints, and maintenance troubleshooting.

STEP-BY-STEP: VIEWING THE AUDIT TRAIL

Open ProAccess SPACE
1. Open ProAccess SPACE in your web browser and log in.

Find the Door
2. From the dashboard, click on "Doors" or "Access Points" in the left
   menu.

3. Search for the door by room number or door name. Click on it to open
   its details.

Open the Audit Trail
4. In the door's detail page, look for the "Audit Trail" or "Events"
   tab or button. Click on it.

5. Some versions of ProAccess SPACE show this under "Monitoring" in
   the main menu instead.

Select the Date Range
6. Set the start date and end date for the time period you want to
   review. For example, if a guest reports a problem last night, set
   the range to cover that night.

7. Click "Search" or "Apply" to load the events for that period.

Reading the Audit Trail Log
8. The log shows events in chronological order. Each entry includes:
   - Date and time of the event.
   - Cardholder name (who presented the card).
   - Event type: "Access Granted," "Access Denied," "Door Opened,"
     "Door Left Open," "Passage Mode On/Off," etc.
   - Reason for denial if applicable (expired card, wrong zone,
     low battery, etc.).

9. Look for patterns. For example:
   - Multiple "Access Denied" entries may indicate a bad card or
     wrong permissions.
   - "Door Left Open" alerts may point to a hardware issue.
   - Unknown cardholder names could indicate a security concern.

Export the Audit Trail
10. To save or print the log, look for an "Export" or "Download" button.

11. Choose your preferred format (CSV or PDF are common options).

12. Save the file to your computer. This is useful for attaching to
    incident reports or emailing to management.

TIPS
- If the audit trail is empty for an offline lock, the events are stored
  in the lock itself and will upload once the lock reconnects or is
  updated with the PPD.
- Audit trail access may be restricted based on your user role. If you
  cannot see the audit trail, ask your manager or system administrator.
- For a cardholder-based view (everywhere a specific person went), look
  for an audit option on the cardholder's profile page instead.
