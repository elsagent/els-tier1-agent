SALTO Electronic Lock - Clock Sync Guide
=========================================

WHAT IS CLOCK DRIFT?
Every SALTO lock has a small internal clock that keeps track of the date
and time. Over time, this clock can slowly drift and become inaccurate,
sometimes by several minutes or even hours. This is called clock drift.

WHY CLOCK DRIFT CAUSES PROBLEMS
Guest keycards are programmed with specific valid-from and valid-until
times. If the lock's internal clock is wrong, it may think a card has
expired early or has not started yet, even when the card is valid.

Common symptoms of clock drift:
- Guests get "access denied" (red flash) even though their card dates
  are correct.
- Cards work on some doors but not others (different locks have drifted
  by different amounts).
- Cards stop working a few hours before the expected check-out time.

HOW TO SYNC THE LOCK CLOCK

Using the PPD (Portable Programming Device)
1. Connect the PPD to a computer running ProAccess SPACE via USB cable.

2. In ProAccess SPACE, go to the PPD management section. This is
   usually under "Tools" or "Devices" in the menu.

3. Click "Download" or "Update PPD." This loads the correct current
   time and any pending door updates onto the PPD.

4. Safely disconnect the PPD from the computer.

5. Take the PPD to each affected lock. Hold the PPD against the lock's
   card reader.

6. Wait for the transfer to complete. The lock will typically flash or
   beep to confirm it received the update. The PPD screen will show a
   confirmation.

7. Repeat for every lock that needs syncing.

Using Wireless (Online Locks Only)
8. If the lock is online and connected wirelessly, the clock should
   sync automatically with the server.

9. In ProAccess SPACE, find the door and check its "Last Communication"
   timestamp. If it is recent, the clock should already be accurate.

10. If the timestamp is old, the lock may have lost its wireless
    connection. See the Lock Offline guide for troubleshooting.

BEST PRACTICES
- Sync lock clocks at least once per month to prevent drift from
  building up.
- After a daylight saving time change, sync all locks promptly.
- If you notice multiple guests getting denied at the same door,
  check that door's clock first.
- Keep the PPD charged and updated so it is always ready for use.

WHEN TO ESCALATE
- If a lock's clock keeps drifting rapidly after syncing (more than a
  few minutes per week), the lock hardware may be faulty. Escalate to
  Tier 2 support.
